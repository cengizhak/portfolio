01 — Overview

The problem with group travel

Planning a group trip is exciting — but the process is fragmented. Conversations happen across WhatsApp, spreadsheets, and emails. Preferences get lost. Decisions take forever. And someone always feels unheard.

Travely is a mobile app designed to centralise the entire group planning journey: from collecting preferences to generating AI-assisted itinerary options the whole group can review, vote on, and refine together.

Design questions

"How might we make group travel decisions easier and more transparent for everyone involved?"

"How might we support collaborative planning without relying on messy chat discussions?"

03 — Research

What we found

Mixed-methods research combining a 16-question online survey with qualitative interviews revealed three core pain clusters.

🔔

Notification overload

Travellers were drowning in updates — yet still missing critical changes. Too much noise, not enough signal. Apps weren't helping users distinguish urgent from routine alerts.

👥

Group coordination friction

Groups defaulted to chat threads for planning — but chat is ephemeral and non-structured. Preferences were raised, forgotten, and re-raised. No single source of truth existed.

⚡

Disruption handling

When itineraries changed, users had to manually find alternatives across multiple apps. Rebooking a flight meant leaving the planning context entirely — causing stress and decision fatigue.

04 — Insights

From observations to design direction

  • 01Group members want input, but don't want to manage the process
  • 02Transparency in decisions reduces conflict and builds trust
  • 03Users want smart defaults, not endless configuration
  • 04A shared space replaces scattered threads only if it's frictionless to adopt
  • 05The moment of "finalising" the trip is emotionally significant — design should honour it

05 — Solution

The design concept

Travely's core loop: collect → generate → decide → confirm. Each step is structured, visible, and shared with the group.

Core flow

Preference-to-itinerary pipeline

The trip coordinator creates a structured questionnaire covering travel type, budget, accommodation, and duration. Responses feed into two AI-generated itinerary options: a majority-based plan and a balanced plan that honours minority preferences.

🗳️

Transparent group decisions

Every member votes on the final itinerary. Results are visible to all — no more "who decided this?" The voting system builds trust and shared ownership of the trip.

🏠

Dedicated trip workspace

Each trip gets its own space: voting, budget tracker, group chat, and a shared inspiration board. One URL replaces five different apps and three chat groups.

📄

Auto-generated trip PDF

After confirmation, the app generates a complete itinerary PDF with tickets, reservations, emergency contacts, and calendar integration — shareable with the whole group instantly.

🗺️

Information architecture

23-slide deck with a site map spanning 7 top-level sections and 4 depth levels — designed around mental models revealed in research, not feature lists.

08 — Reflection

What I took away

What worked well

The mixed-methods approach (survey + interviews) created a rich picture quickly. The user journey map became the clearest communication tool in the final presentation — both for the team and the audience.

What I learned

Structuring group dynamics in a UI is fundamentally different from individual flows. The challenge wasn't the features — it was designing for asymmetric roles (coordinator vs. member) within the same interface.

What's next

Usability testing on the voting flow and the questionnaire UX. The AI-balanced itinerary concept needs validation — do users trust it? Do minority preferences actually feel represented?
